MY TV-SHOWS
"I’ve watched so many TV shows that I’ve lost count! Most of them are K-dramas, along with a mix of other Asian series. I do watch Western shows too, but only if they really intrigue me. On this page, you’ll find a collection of my all-time favorites—each one earning a solid 5 stars in my book! They’re neatly organized by genre, making it easier to see what I love most. Whether it’s heart-fluttering romances, thrilling mysteries, or mind-blowing sci-fi, these are the shows that have left a lasting impression on me."
FAVORITES ROMANCE FANTASY OTHERS
Top Favorite Of All-Time
Stranger Things


Director:The Duffer Brothers
Genre: Science Fiction, Horror, Mystery, Supernatural

           I watched Stranger Things during the pandemic out of pure curiosity, and it turned out to be one of the most mind-blowing shows I’ve ever seen. The mix of 80s nostalgia, supernatural mysteries, and incredible storytelling kept me hooked from start to finish. Every twist and turn had me at the edge of my seat, and the characters felt so real. I even watched the whole series in one sitting because I couldn’t stop! Out of all the seasons, Season 4 is my absolute favorite.

Girl From Nowhere


Director: Khom Kongkiat Khomsiri, Sittisiri Mongkolsiri
Genre: Thriller, Mystery, Drama, Supernatural

         I discovered Girl from Nowhere during the pandemic as well, and it was the first ever Thai drama I watched. The show had such a crazy theory back then, and its concept was incredibly unique. Every episode was unpredictable and unforgettable, tackling dark themes in a thought-provoking way. It kept me hooked the entire time, making it one of the most memorable series I’ve seen.

When I Fly Towards You

Director: Mao De Shu
Genre: Romance, Youth, Friendship, School


         When I Fly Towards You is my ultimate Chinese drama. While watching it, it gave me a certain nostalgia for youth, making me wish I had a love like theirs—no angst, just pure fluff and wholesome moments.

         More than just the romance, I found myself wishing I had a group of friends like them. This show is just so comforting to me, like a warm hug that I could watch over and over again.

Hotel Del Luna


Director: Oh Choong-hwan
Genre: Fantasy, Romance, Supernatural, Horror

         Hotel Del Luna is a drama I never thought would become one of my top favorites—but of course, it’s IU! I really love K-dramas like this, where the concept is so creative and unique. Every episode felt like a beautiful blend of mystery, emotion, and breathtaking visuals. This drama does have horror elements, though not extreme, just enough to keep things thrilling without being too scary. And IU’s wardrobe in this drama? Absolutely phenomenal!

Attack On Titan

Creator: Hajime Isayama
Genre: Action, Dark Fantasy, Post-Apocalyptic

         Attack on Titan is hands down the best and most phenomenal, out-of-this-universe series I have ever watched. Although it's an anime, this is the best out of all the best. The storyline, the way everything is intricately connected—there is no single plot hole. Every detail, every twist, and every revelation is mind-blowing. Of course, the gore is evident, but beyond the bloodshed and battles, this series is so much more. I just want to thank Hajime Isayama for creating this masterpiece. I must say, it’s not just about killing titans; it’s about humanity, freedom, and the weight of choices.

Go-To-Top
Romance
Twinkling Watermelon

Director: Son Jung-hyun
Genre: Fantasy, Drama, Romance, Time Travel

         Twinkling Watermelon is a drama that healed me in so many ways. Although it’s a mix of fantasy with the leads traveling through time, it carries a deep emotional weight. It’s traumatizing in some aspects, yet at the same time, it brings a sense of tranquility. I love how they highlight the importance of expressing oneself, which made the story even more touching—especially considering the irony that she is mute. This drama made me cry so much, yet in some way, it healed a part of me. I just wish I could watch it again for the first time. It’s somewhat devastating, yet so beautifully written.

         After watching this, I became completely addicted to the phrase "Viva La Vida"—it just resonates so much with the emotions of the show. I even started buying watermelon-themed stuff just to hold onto that connection. Also, I absolutely love how youthful this drama feels. It’s filled with moments of joy, passion, and nostalgia, making it even more unforgettable.

Weightlifting Fairy Kim Bok Joo

Director: Oh Hyun-jong
Genre: Romance, Comedy, Sports, Coming-of-Age

         Weightlifting Fairy Kim Bok-joo was the very first K-drama I ever watched—I was probably around 14 years old when I saw it, back when the Hallyu wave of K-dramas really started to take off. Oh, how I wish I could watch this for the first time again! It’s a very corny drama, but that’s exactly what made it so endearing. The humor, the odd but lovable characters, and the nonstop fun—there was never a dull moment.

         And let’s not forget the romance! I was absolutely devastated when the on-screen couple broke up in real life, but that doesn’t take away how comforting this show is to me. It’s the kind of drama that just makes you feel warm and happy, like a little slice of nostalgia I’ll always cherish.

A Love So Beautiful

Director: Yang Long
Genre: Romance, Youth, Comedy, Coming-of-Age

A Love So Beautiful is one of those dramas I can never get tired of—I’ve watched it at least four times because I just can’t get enough of it! I always have a thing for cold male leads who eventually soften up later, and this drama delivers that dynamic perfectly.

         What I love most about this Chinese drama is how purely high school it feels. The youthful energy, the innocence, and the simplicity of their love story make it such a comforting watch. It’s vibrant and lighthearted, without unnecessary complications. Just pure wholesomeness, and that’s exactly why I keep coming back to it. No matter how many times I’ve seen it, I will never get tired of rewatching this again and again. It just has that nostalgic and heartwarming charm that makes me want to relive it every single time.

Lovely Runner

Director: Yoon Jong-ho
Genre: Romance, Fantasy, Drama, Time-Travel

         Lovely Runner is my recent top romance series, and I will never get tired of recommending it to certain people. I absolutely love the storyline—it has so many elements mixed in, but I’ll just place it under my romance section.

         The male lead, Byeon Woo Seok, completely caught my attention, and I was obsessed with every episode. I remember when this was airing, I would anxiously wait for each new release, and the cliffhangers were driving me crazy! This is one of those dramas I wish I could watch again for the first time. Every time someone asks me about it, and they have no clue what it’s about, I get so jealous because they get to experience it fresh.

Hidden Love

Director: Lee Qingrong
Genre: Romance, Youth, Drama, Coming-of-Age

         Hidden Love is a drama I always find myself thinking about. I regret waiting a whole year after its release to finally watch it because I had uncertainties, but oh boy, how I wish I had watched it sooner!

         This has become another one of my ultimate comfort shows, and it heals me in so many different ways. The way the leads handle each other, the pacing of their love story—everything about it feels so sincere and natural. It’s a long journey, but it’s worth every single moment.

Go-To-Top
Fantasy
Alchemy Of Souls

Genre: Fantasy, Historical, Romance


      I was never really a big fan of historical dramas, but Alchemy of Souls completely changed my mind. The flow of the story, the intricate world-building, and the different versions of Naksu— yet he fell in love with every version of her. This drama is honestly one of the best, and if I ever got amnesia, this would be the first thing I’d rewatch. I still remember losing sleep just to binge this series. To be honest, I preferred Season 2, and I know some might disagree, but oh, how I wish I could experience this for the first time again.

Light Shop

Genre: Horror, Fantasy, Mystery


      Light Shop was honestly terrifying at first because of its horror elements, but as the story unraveled, it became something much deeper— a tale about life, death, and the weight of guilt and regrets. The characters were trapped in the same alley, unable to move forward, and the Light Shop served as the final decision—whether to continue or not. I really loved the theme and concept; it was so unique that it got me completely hooked. I watched it all in one sitting, and yet, I’m still devastated that the couple didn’t recognize each other in the end.

Moving

Director: Park In-je
Genre: Action, Supernatural, Thriller

     Moving is just a masterpiece—just a set of people with supernatural powers, yet it felt so real. I watched this in one sitting without any interruptions because I was completely hooked. I absolutely love their dynamic, especially the deep and emotional parent-child relationships.

     It also made me wonder—what if there are actually people out there with powers, secretly working for the government? That thought alone kept me thinking even after finishing the show. I’m so glad I watched this!

Uncanny Counter

Director: Yoo Seon-dong (Season 1), Yoo Seon-dong & Kim Sae-bom (Season 2)
Genre: Action, Fantasy, Supernatural

      Uncanny Counter is such a thrilling series, and I have to say—I loved Season 2 even more! It leans heavily on action, but I enjoyed every bit of it. The whole concept of them being "the chosen ones" makes them an amazing team, and I absolutely adore their dynamic.

     The curly-haired family is just everything, and I truly hope we get a Season 3 soon because I’m tired of waiting. And please, just let them be together already!

Alice in Borderland

Director: Shinsuke Sato
Genre: Thriller, Sci-Fi, Survival, Mystery

      Alice in Borderland is one of the most mind-bending Japanese dramas I’ve ever watched. It’s confusing at times, but I love the theory that the games represent the battle between life and death, where the players are literally fighting for their will to live.

     My favorite episode was, of course, the intense battle with the King of Spades—absolutely thrilling! But let’s be real, there is no way I would ever survive these games. I would have died on the spot. Now, I can’t wait for Season 3, and I really hope we finally get to see the Joker this time!

Others
Taxi Driver

Genre: Action, Thriller, Crime
Director: Park Joon-woo

      It took me years to finally finish Taxi Driver, and I have no idea why I waited so long. I finally knocked some sense into myself and gave it the attention it deserved.

     As a sucker for thriller and action, this drama instantly became my standard for the genre. Every episode was a chef’s kiss—intense, gripping, and impossible to stop watching. What makes it even more powerful is how eye-opening and relevant it is. The themes explored in this drama aren’t just fiction; they reflect real-life issues that make you think, question, and even wish for justice in the same way.

Mouse

Genre: Thriller, Crime, Mystery
Director: Choi Joon-bae, Kang Cheol-woo

     Nothing will ever beat Mouse when it comes to plot twists after plot twists. My jaw dropped at every climax—I just couldn’t comprehend what was happening. The cast delivered an amazing performance, making every moment even more intense. I would honestly be jealous of anyone who still hasn’t watched this masterpiece, because experiencing it for the first time is a wild ride.

      I binge-watched this at midnight and finished it the next morning— that’s how gripping it was. And I have to give major credit to the writer; the storytelling in this show is simply out of this world.

Demon Slayer

Genre: Action, Adventure, Fantasy
Director: Haruo Sotozaki

     I know that Demon Slayer is a mainstream favorite, but I genuinely love it because it’s not complicated—just straight to the point. The animation in this show is simply incredible. I have so much appreciation for the amazing animators who brought this world to life. Every battle scene, every visual—it’s just breathtaking.

     My favorite arc has to be the Entertainment District Arc. The sudden burst of colors, the festival-like atmosphere, and the intensity of the fights— it was truly a spectacle. I’m already beyond excited for the next and final arc, but at the same time, I just wish this series would never end.

Black Clover

Genre: Action, Adventure, Fantasy
Director: Tatsuya Yoshihara, Ayataka Tanemura

      Black Clover is another masterpiece that I truly enjoyed watching, and it easily made its way into my top favorite animes. This anime has over 200+ episodes, and I still can’t believe I watched it all— especially knowing that my patience is as thin as a strand of hair.

     One thing I absolutely love about this show is the empowerment. The way the writer highlights and gives so much importance to the female characters is something I truly admire. I also love the character development of every single character. Watching them grow stronger—physically, mentally, and emotionally— made the journey even more exciting and satisfying. If I had to choose a favorite character, it would definitely be Charlotte—but of course, I also have a soft spot for Asta.

From Me To You (Kimi Ni Todoke)

Genre: Romance, Slice of Life, Drama
Director: Hiro Kaburagi

         If someone ever asks me for an anime recommendation with a slow-burn romance, Kimi ni Todoke is always my go-to suggestion. I have a serious love-hate relationship with this anime— there are moments when I feel like the characters are moving way too slow, but at the same time, the gradual development just feels so right. It’s as if the story is gently reminding us to take our time with love.

         This anime is also one of my ultimate comfort shows. With its high school setting, it gives off such a strong feeling of nostalgia and a beautifully innocent youthful love. It’s the kind of story that makes me appreciate the simplicity of young romance— slow, sweet, and filled with heartwarming moments.
